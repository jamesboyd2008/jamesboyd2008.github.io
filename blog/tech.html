How does the internet work?

February 1, 2016

The internet is a large network of computers, somewhat like a highway system. Information we pass over the internet is broken down into many tiny packets, which are reassembled at the destination. Computers translate these packets into electronic signals, pass them to a modem, which passes them across wires or through the air via electromagnetic radiation (or light, when fiber optics are involved) to a router. The router directs the packets, through your internet service provider (ISP (comcast, cox, time warner, whatever)) to servers, which are where the web pages are stored. Servers have specific internet protocol (IP) addresses and are connected directly to the internet. Really, everything that is connected to the internet has an IP address. Usually there are a bunch of routers between you and the server with which you are communicating. Those servers connect, via more routers and ISPs, to another end client, which, at one point or another, has another human connected to it by a mouse and keyboard!
